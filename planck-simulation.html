About this simulation: This uses Monte Carlo rejection sampling to generate photons according to Planck's spectral emissive power law: Eλ(λ,T) = (2πhc²/λ⁵) × 1/(ehc/λkBT - 1). The theoretical Planck curve (black line) is always shown. As photons accumulate, the blue histogram columns converge to this theoretical distribution.

Convergence metric: The plot below shows the Mean Squared Error (MSE) between the normalized Monte Carlo histogram and the normalized theoretical Planck distribution. Both distributions are normalized to sum to 1 (probability densities) before comparison, which allows proper convergence tracking regardless of the total number of photons. As this MSE value decreases toward zero, the simulation better matches the theory. The convergence follows approximately a 1/√N behavior, where N is the number of photons, meaning you need 4× more photons to halve the error.
